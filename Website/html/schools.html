Schools I Have Attended

Eagle Plains Junior Public School

Grade 1-2

When I started school the plan was to go into french immersion.
There were no schools near where I lived that had french immersion except Eagle Plains, home of the Eagles (Obviously).
I remember Eagle Plains being a lot of fun for me.


Springdale Public School

Grade 2-3

After joining Eagle Plains for a year us french immersion students were moved to Springdale, home of the dragons, which hosted the school that we would be moving to in grade 3, Carberry.
Springdale was a good school, and I remember being on the soccer team there.
Students who were going to Carberry after their year in Springdale got to vote on the mascot of Carberry.
I remember voting for Carberry Cardinals, though Carberry Cougars ended up winning.


Carberry Public School

Grade 3-5

Finally after two years of waiting the french immersion kids were moved into a brand new school, Carberry.
Carberry had a lot of things to do, In carberry I was part of the soccer team as well as the step team.
I was also part of the choir, where we got to sing at brampton hockey games once a year.


Earnscliffe Senior Public School

Grade 6-8

Throughout middle school I went to Earnscliffe Senior Public School. Earnscliffe was really fun and had a lot of extra-curricular activities.
Earnscliffe was where I learned to play the alto saxophone in band, which I was in thoughout my three years of middle school. One of my favourite extra-curricular activities was CYOB or Create Your Own Business where students had to make businesses and sell their products to other students as well as adults.
The school took a cut of the money you made, but if your product was good you would still profit.


Bramalea Secondary School

Grade 9-12

For my four years of high school I attended Bramalea Secondary School. Though Bramalea had a lot to offer with tons of clubs and groups to choose from, the only club I joined was band, because I was busy with work at the time, as a swim instructor. I joined band to continue learning the alto saxophone along with some of my friends from middle school. My four years at Bramalea were what you would expect from a typical high school, and my favourite year was easily grade 12, where I got more free time due to spares.


Brock University

Years 2+

My first year at the village residence at Brock was an amazing year, before covid-19, and was one of the best years of my life. I plan to stay at Brock and continue to study computer science for the next two and a half years until I am done my major. So far I have not had any issues with Brock and look forward to a time when the campus will be open and classes are in person again.
